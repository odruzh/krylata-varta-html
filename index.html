Residents of our reserve

Crow

A crow is a bird of the genus Corvus, or more broadly a synonym for all of Corvus. The term "crow" is used as part of the common name of many species.

Greenfinch

The greenfinches are small passerine birds in the genus Chloris in the subfamily Carduelinae within the Fringillidae.

Sparrow

Sparrows are seed-eating birds with conical bills, brown or gray in color, and many species have distinctive head patterns.

Starling

Starlings are small to medium-sized passerine birds in the family Sturnidae. The name "Sturnidae" comes from the Latin word for starling, sturnus.

Tomtit

The tomtit is a small (13 cm, 11 g) bird with a large head and a short bill. The male North Island subspecies has black head, back, wings (with a white wing bar) and a white belly.
